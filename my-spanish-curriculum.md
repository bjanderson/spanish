## Pronunciation

Pronunciation is an important part of any language, but it's particularly important in Spanish because some words change meaning when you pronounce them differently.
Spanish is also a phonetic language, which basically means that it is written exactly how it sounds - so if you know how to pronounce things correctly, you can speak proper Spanish just by reading things out loud.
When it comes to pronunciation, Spanish is actually a very straight-forward, uncomplicated language - especially compared to English.

I'm starting this curriculum with pronunciation because it's better for you to have that in mind up front, rather than form any bad habits that you will have to unlearn later.
Start off trying to pronounce everything correctly, and you will learn and advance much faster and easier.

## Gender

Gender is an important part of the Spanish language.
Almost every noun (person/place/object/thing) is spoken with a gender - whether it actually has a gender or not.
There are exceptions to the rule, but masculine nouns typically end in "o" and feminine nouns typically end in "a".

If a noun is masculine the word for "the" that comes before it will be masculine (el -singular, los - plural)
If a noun is feminine the word for "the" that comes before it will be feminine (la -singular, las - plural)

"The table" (la mesa) has a feminine gender just like "the girl" (la chica).
"The book" (el libro) has a masculine gender just like "the boy" (el chico).

If you talk about a group of something that has both genders in it, you will refer to it as masculine.
For example, if you were talking about a group of men and women you would say "they" (ellos) in the masculine form - even if there is only one man in the group.
You will only say "they" (ellas) in the feminine form only if there were no men in the group.

They are so common that we kind of take them for granted.
Here's a list of some of those common words to get you started.
You don't really have to worry about knowing any rules about these words.
Just memorize them and use them as a foundation to build on.
